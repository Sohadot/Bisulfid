# Bisulfid vs Bisulfide: The Missing Letter

The domain is bisulfid.com — not bisulfide.com. The difference is a single letter: the final E.

That letter is not missing. It belongs to the English form.

---

## Two Spellings, One Compound

Bisulfide and Bisulfid refer to the same underlying compound. The difference in spelling reflects a systematic pattern in how German and English chemical nomenclature handle the suffix for this compound class.

- **English form:** bisulf**ide**
- **German form:** bisulf**id**

This is not an error in the domain name. It is the point of the domain name.

---

## The E Is Not Missing

A common misreading of bisulfid.com is that the domain has a typo — that the E was accidentally dropped.

The E was not dropped. The E belongs to the English spelling of the compound. The German spelling does not use it.

The domain chooses the German form deliberately. That choice is the conceptual architecture of the asset.
